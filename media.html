In this podcast, titled "hidden history", a local historian discusses the history of the Knockmarie Dolmen. Details about the probable origin of the tomb and its likley occupants are discussed. Directions to the tomb are also given.

Locals celibrate the annual Chapelizod community festival with music, dancing and singing. The festival has been running every year without fail since 1982. The highligh of the festival each year is the James Joyce walk - a celebration of the fact that James Joyce himself freqented Chapelizod.

A peaceful walk along the river liffey. The town is intertwined by the Liffey, no matter where you go, you can see it! This tour is in one of the more peaceful locations. North of the village. This section is home to many many wildlife species. If you are lucky you will spot otters and herons. If you are really lucky you will see a seal known locally as "Jim". He is known to swim all the way from Dublin bay!
